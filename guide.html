Maze Solving Rulebook

1. Purpose of the Maze

The objective of this maze is to identify a continuous, valid path from the designated starting point to the designated exit point by navigating through interconnected corridors while strictly following the defined rules. The maze tests logical reasoning, spatial awareness, and problem-solving skills.

2. Maze Structure

  • The maze consists of interconnected pathways separated by solid walls
  • All pathways are of uniform width
  • All walls are impassable
  • The maze has one valid entry point and one valid exit point
  • No hidden passages, movable walls, or shortcuts exist

3. Starting and Ending Rules

  • The solver must begin at the marked starting position
  • The solver must finish at the designated exit point
  • Entering the maze from any other location is not permitted
  • Exiting the maze at any point other than the designated exit is invalid

4. Movement Rules

  • Movement is allowed only through open pathways
  • The solver may move forward, backward, left, or right
  • Diagonal movement is strictly prohibited
  • The solver must remain within maze corridors at all times

5. Prohibited Actions (Don’ts)

  • ❌ Crossing, touching, or cutting through walls
  • ❌ Lifting writing tools to skip sections (if physically solving)
  • ❌ Marking or altering maze walls
  • ❌ Creating new paths or modifying existing ones
  • ❌ Using external aids unless explicitly allowed

6. Allowed Actions (Do’s)

  • ✔ Tracing the path continuously within corridors
  • ✔ Backtracking when a dead end is reached
  • ✔ Pausing and resuming, provided continuity is maintained
  • ✔ Using logical strategies such as wall-following

7. Solution Validity Criteria

A solution is considered valid only if:

  • The path begins at the official start point
  • The path ends at the official exit point
  • The path remains entirely within maze corridors
  • No walls are crossed or violated
  • All movement rules are followed

8. Time and Attempts

  • The maze may be solved with or without a time limit
  • Multiple attempts are allowed unless stated otherwise
  • Only the final completed path is evaluated

9. Ethical and Fair Play Guidelines

  • Solvers must rely on their own reasoning and skill
  • Copying pre-solved paths or automated solvers is discouraged
  • Fair play and honesty are expected

10. Conclusion

This maze is designed to promote logical thinking, patience, and structured problem-solving. Successful completion depends on careful navigation, adherence to rules, and disciplined exploration.
